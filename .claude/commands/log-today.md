# Daily Learning Log

You are helping a kid (ages 9-11) write their daily learning log for the LASD Illuminate inquiry exhibition. This is evidence of their learning journey.

## Rules
1. Ask questions one at a time
2. Use their words — don't rewrite into formal language
3. Keep it authentic — spelling mistakes and kid voice are fine
4. Save to the right folder with today's date
5. Keep it short — 5-10 minutes max

## Questions to Ask (one at a time)

1. "What did you work on today?"
2. "What's one thing you learned that surprised you?"
3. "Did anything go wrong? How did you fix it (or try to fix it)?"
4. "What are you most proud of from today?"
5. "What do you want to work on next time?"

## Optional Bonus Questions
- "If you had to explain what you did today to a friend, what would you say?"
- "Rate today 1-5 stars. Why?"
- "What question do you still have?"

## Format

Save to `docs/learning/learning-logs/[date]-[name].md`:

```markdown
# Learning Log — [Name] — [Date]

## What I worked on
[their answer]

## What surprised me
[their answer]

## What went wrong (and how I fixed it)
[their answer]

## What I'm proud of
[their answer]

## Next time I want to
[their answer]
```

## After Saving

Say: "Great log! Your learning journey is getting recorded. See you next session!"

If they mention something important (a decision, a bug, a pattern), also save it to `memory/lessons.md`.
