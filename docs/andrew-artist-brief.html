The Game

SPLIT
You're a jello cube. You're trapped. And the only way out is UP. Fight, split, and craft your way through a monster-filled castle — upgrading your skills floor by floor until you're strong enough to face what waits at the top. In Split, every piece of you matters.

Genre: 2D side-scrolling platformer (like Mario meets Dead Cells)

Art Style: Dead Cells pixel art — small pixels, but clean and readable shapes. NOT muddy like Minecraft pixels.

Mood: Dark but not scary. Mysterious. Alternates between peaceful exploration and intense combat.

Players: Single player

School-appropriate: No weapons, no blood. Everything is jello-based.

The World

You're inside a massive castle in the middle of a desert. The game starts in the dungeon and goes UP floor by floor.

Castle Interior:

  • Old stone walls covered in creeping vines
  • Torches on walls providing warm light
  • Occasional windows — light comes through but you CAN'T see outside
  • A creeping “gloom” darkness that takes over parts of the castle
  • Less light = more dangerous areas
  • Chests to open, barrels to break
  • Cooking pots (where you craft health)
  • Levers and pressure plates (hidden puzzle elements)
  • Glowing door at the end of each level (portal to next floor)

Outside (title screen only):

  • Desert landscape surrounding the castle
  • The castle itself — tall, dark, imposing

The Character

A jello cube with little eyeballs.

  • Transparent/translucent body
  • Small cute eyeballs
  • Jiggly — wobbles when moving, squishes on landing
  • Items are stored INSIDE the body (you can see water bottles, pills, shields floating inside)
  • Changes color when using pills (e.g., turns orange with fire pill)
  • Can split into fourths (4 smaller cubes)
  • Gets bigger when crafting health (jello powder + water)

Your Task: Title Screen Design

This is what we need from you, Andrew. Design the title screen for Split.

What the Title Screen Should Include:

1. The word "SPLIT" in big pixel-style letters at the top

  • Should feel bold and memorable
  • Pixel art style matching the Dead Cells aesthetic

2. The jello cube character doing a pose

  • Front and center
  • Showing personality — maybe looking determined, or mid-jiggle
  • Transparent body with eyeballs visible

3. A detailed background scene

  • The castle with the desert behind it
  • MORE detailed than the actual gameplay (like how Minecraft's menu has a 3D world, or Zelda shows Link in a detailed scene)
  • Moody lighting — torches, shadows, maybe a sunset desert sky

4. Three buttons (we'll add these digitally):

  • Play
  • Settings
  • Controls

References to Look At:

  • Dead Cells title screen — for pixel art style and mood
  • Zelda: Breath of the Wild title screen — for the detailed background with character
  • Minecraft main menu — for how the background is more detailed than gameplay
  • Celeste title screen — for a clean, bold game title

Deliverable:

  • A drawn/sketched title screen design
  • Can be pencil, colored pencil, marker, digital — whatever you want
  • Show where the title goes, where the character is, what the background looks like
  • Include color notes if drawing in pencil (e.g., “dark blue sky”, “orange torch glow”)
  • If you have ideas for the character design (the jello cube), sketch those too!

Important Notes:

  • The art style is pixel art (Dead Cells), so think in terms of clean shapes and readable silhouettes
  • The mood is dark/mysterious but NOT horror — more like a spooky adventure
  • The jello cube should feel lovable and determined, not scared
  • Everything should be school-appropriate
