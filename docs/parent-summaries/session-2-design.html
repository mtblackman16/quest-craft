Quest Craft — Session 2 Recap

The Deep Dive: Designing the Full Game
March 8, 2026  |  Ethan, Eins, Nathan & Andrew
The team designed an entire video game in one session.
15 castle floors, 3 bosses, 4 difficulty modes, a complete combat system, and a world buried under the desert — all from the kids' imaginations.

What They Built Today

  • 7 complete design documents — Characters, Story/World, Gameplay Mechanics, Levels, Art Style, Sound/Music, and Controls. Every aspect of the game is now documented.
  • A 15-floor castle with distinct zones: exploration floors, parkour speed floors with crumbling platforms and lasers, a deadly slow-paced gauntlet, and an epic 3-phase final boss.
  • 3 unique enemies and 3 bosses — each with distinct attack patterns, weaknesses, and a "dodge, learn, punish" design philosophy inspired by Hollow Knight and Dead Cells.
  • A mass-based combat system where your health IS your ammo — every attack costs body mass, creating constant risk/reward decisions. Plus a cooking system where you craft health from jello powder + water at cooking pots.
  • "Earthquake Mode" — an extreme difficulty where the castle collapses around you, platforms never respawn, and beating it unlocks a secret ending teasing a sequel.

Skills Practiced

Game Design
Designed combat mechanics, enemy behaviors, boss fights, difficulty curves, and level progression from scratch
Systems Thinking
Created interconnected systems: mass = health = ammo, cooking for healing, elemental pills from shrines, environmental hazards
Collaborative Decision-Making
Every design choice was discussed as a team — Nathan noted "everybody's ideas are very important to the project being successful"
Creative Storytelling
Designed environmental storytelling — no text dumps, players discover the castle's history by exploring
Technical Literacy
Mapped every game action to specific controller buttons, understanding hardware-software connections
Version Control
Learned that committing and pushing saves work — Ethan and Andrew both highlighted this as a key lesson
Illuminate Connection: Today was pure design thinking in action. The team took a vague concept ("jello cube in a castle") and systematically broke it down into characters, mechanics, levels, art direction, sound, and controls — exactly the kind of structured creative problem-solving that Illuminate showcases. Every design document they wrote today becomes part of their exhibition story: "Here's HOW we designed a game."
