Our Learning Journey

Our Inquiry Question
What can kids create when they have AI as an engineering partner?
Phase 1 - Investigate
What did we investigate and why?
Can four kids (ages 9-12) design and build a real video game using AI as their engineering partner?
"Kids can design a game that can be extremely successful since kids play games themselves and have great taste in games."
- Eins, 12
Phase 2 - Discover
What did we learn?
Game design is harder than coding. The AI can write any code, but only if you describe what you want clearly. Learning to say "the jump feels too floaty" instead of "it's broken" was the biggest skill we developed.
"How quickly Claude could understand us and create a functioning game, even if it isn't perfect."
- Andrew, 12
Phase 3 - Reflect
How did we learn it?
We followed 5 sessions: Dream, Design, Build, Test, Ship. We talked to AI like a teammate, played after every change, and gave honest feedback. Speak, change, test - that loop is how we learned everything.
"You need context. If you just say 'make a game', then it wouldn't know what game to make."
- Ethan, 9
Phase 4 - Share
What are we sharing today?
A real, playable game - 15 floors, 5 bosses, running on a Raspberry Pi. And the story of how 4 kids built it with AI.
"Being able to create the game and show it to the people at the exhibition."
- Nathan, 9
