# Session 1: DREAM — The Vision

**Goal:** Define what game you're making. Crystal clear vision that the whole team agrees on.

**Time:** ~2 hours

**What you need:** Your voice (Wispr Flow), paper, pencils/markers, your imagination

**Slash command:** `/dream`

---

## How This Session Works

You're going to have a conversation with Claude — your AI teammate. Claude will ask you questions about your game idea. You just TALK. Speak naturally, like you're telling a friend about the coolest game ever. Claude listens, asks follow-up questions, and writes everything down.

**Important:** There are NO wrong answers. This is YOUR game. Dream big!

---

## The Conversation

Claude will guide you through these questions (one at a time, going deeper on each):

### 1. The Big Idea
"If you could play ANY game right now that doesn't exist yet, what would it be like?"

### 2. The Genre
"Is it more like Zelda (adventure), Minecraft (building), Mario (platformer), a fighting game, or something totally new?"

### 3. The Hook
"What's the ONE thing that makes your game different from every other game?"

### 4. The Experience
"Imagine showing this to your friends at school. What do they say when they first see it?"

### 5. The Feeling
"When someone plays your game, how should they feel? Excited? Scared? Like a hero? Like a genius?"

### 6. Win and Lose
"How do you win? How do you lose? What keeps you coming back to play again?"

### 7. Inspiration
"What games, movies, or shows inspired this idea? What's the best part of each one?"

### 8. The Pitch
Claude will summarize everything back to you: "So your game is [DESCRIPTION]. Is that right? What would you change?"

### 9. The Title
"If this game was on a store shelf, what would the cover look like? What's it called?"

### 10. Dream Big, Then Focus
"If you had a YEAR to build this game, what would it have? Now — what are the 3 most important things for the version we build in 2 weeks?"

---

## Game Design Wisdom

During this session, Claude will teach you this principle:

**Core Concept & Scope Management** — "Every great game starts with one clear idea. The best games do one thing amazingly well. Minecraft is about building. Mario is about jumping. Zelda is about exploring. What is YOUR game about?"

---

## Creative Activities (Away from the Screen)

After the conversation:

1. **Draw the Title Screen** — On paper, draw what players see when they first start your game. Include the game name, a cool image, and "Press Start."

2. **Write the Back of the Box** — In 2-3 sentences, write the description that would make someone want to play your game. Like the back of a video game case.

3. **Team Vote** — If the team has multiple name ideas, vote! Everyone gets one vote.

## Talking Tips

Not sure what to say? Try these:

- "What if the game was like [GAME] but with [TWIST]?"
- "I think it would be cool if the player could..."
- "The best part about [GAME] is... I want that feeling in our game"
- "What if instead of [X] we did [Y]?"
- "Can you read back what we have so far?"
