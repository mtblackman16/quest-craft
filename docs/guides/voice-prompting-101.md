# Voice Prompting 101 — How to Talk to Claude

You're going to talk to an AI called Claude using a tool called **Wispr Flow**. You just hold a key and speak — Wispr Flow types what you say, and Claude responds.

It's like talking to a really smart teammate who can build anything you describe.

---

## The Basics

### How Wispr Flow Works
1. Put your cursor in the Claude Code terminal
2. Hold the Wispr Flow key (ask Mark which key)
3. Speak naturally — like you're talking to a friend
4. Let go of the key — your words appear as text
5. Press Enter — Claude reads your message and responds

### The Golden Rules
1. **Speak naturally.** Don't try to sound like a robot. Just talk.
2. **One idea at a time.** Don't cram everything into one message.
3. **Be specific.** "Make the jump higher" is better than "fix the jumping."
4. **It's okay to change your mind.** Say "Actually, let's go back to..."
5. **Ask questions!** "Why did you do it that way?" "What does that mean?"

## Power Phrases

These phrases will make your conversations with Claude way more productive:

### When Brainstorming
- "What if we..."
- "I think it would be cool if..."
- "The best part about [GAME] is... I want that feeling"
- "Can you give me three ideas for..."
- "I like that idea but what if we changed..."

### When Designing
- "Go deeper on that — what exactly happens when..."
- "Show me what we've decided so far"
- "I want to change what we said about..."
- "What would YOU suggest?"
- "Read back the [characters/levels/etc.] design"

### When Building
- "Make the [thing] [more/less] [adjective]"
- "Add a [thing] that [does something]"
- "When [this happens], I want [that] to happen"
- "That's close but not quite right. It needs to be more [adjective]"
- "I love that! Don't change it"
- "Save this before we change anything"

### When Debugging
- "Something is wrong — when I [do this], [this happens] instead of [this]"
- "The game crashes when I [exact steps]"
- "This doesn't feel right. The [thing] should be [different]"
- "Can you show me the code that controls [thing]?"

### When Learning
- "Explain that in simpler words"
- "Show me an example"
- "Why did you do it that way instead of [other way]?"
- "I don't understand [concept]. Can you teach me?"
- "That's cool! How does that work?"

### When Stuck
- "I don't know what to say. What should we work on next?"
- "Can you remind me where we left off?"
- "What's left to do?"
- "Give me some options for what to do next"

## Example Conversations

### Starting a Dream Session
> **You:** /dream
>
> **Claude:** "Welcome to the Dream session! Let's figure out what game you're making. If you could play ANY game right now that doesn't exist yet, what would it be like?"
>
> **You:** "I want a game where you're a ninja cat and you have to climb this huge tower and every floor has different enemies and traps"
>
> **Claude:** "A ninja cat tower climb — that sounds awesome! What makes the cat special? Does it have ninja abilities like wall-jumping, throwing stars, or turning invisible?"

### Directing the Build
> **You:** /build
>
> **Claude:** "I just added the player movement. Run the game and try moving around with the left stick."
>
> **You:** "The movement is too floaty. When I let go of the stick, the character keeps sliding. I want it to stop immediately like in Mario."
>
> **Claude:** "Got it — you want snappy, responsive movement. I'll remove the slide and make the player stop instantly when you release the stick. Try it now."

### Reporting a Bug
> **You:** /playtest
>
> **Claude:** "What did you just try?"
>
> **You:** "I was fighting the boss and I jumped on its head, but instead of hurting it, I fell through the floor and died"
>
> **Claude:** "Sounds like a collision detection issue with the boss's hitbox. Let me check... Found it. The boss's collision area was 2 pixels too small. Fixed. Try the boss fight again."

## Tips for Groups

When you're working as a team:
- **Take turns talking to Claude.** Everyone should get a chance to direct.
- **Discuss ideas FIRST, then tell Claude.** It's okay to debate among yourselves!
- **If you disagree, vote.** Majority wins. Claude doesn't pick sides.
- **One person types/talks at a time.** Don't talk over each other.
- **Celebrate each other's ideas.** "That was a great idea, [NAME]!"
